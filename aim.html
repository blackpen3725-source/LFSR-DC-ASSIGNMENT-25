Aim of the Experiment

Objective

To design and simulate a Linear Feedback Shift Register (LFSR) using digital logic circuits and understand its working principle as a pseudo-random sequence generator.

A Linear Feedback Shift Register (LFSR) is a shift register whose input bit is a linear function of its previous state, most commonly implemented using the exclusive-or (XOR) operation.

Why LFSR?

LFSRs generate random-like sequences efficiently and are widely used in cryptography, communication systems, and digital testing.

They support applications such as data encryption, error detection and correction, and digital signature generation.

Tools & Technologies Used

  • HTML5 – Structure and layout.
  • CSS3 – Styling, animation, and 3D effects.
  • JavaScript (ES6) – Interactivity and simulation logic.

Purpose

The virtual lab helps students visualize how an LFSR operates, including feedback, shifting, and sequence generation in a web-based environment.

It offers an interactive platform to design, simulate, and test LFSRs, making complex digital logic concepts easier to grasp.

Applications

  • Cryptography and secure communication.
  • Random number generation.
  • Data scrambling and error detection.
  • Built-in self-test (BIST) for digital circuits.
  • Spread-spectrum communication systems.
  • Digital signature generation.
  • Error correction and detection.

Advantages

  • High speed and efficiency.
  • Low power consumption.
  • Simple and cost-effective implementation.
  • Wide range of applications.

Disadvantages

  • Limited sequence length.
  • Predictable sequence.
  • Not suitable for high-security applications.
